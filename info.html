CATAN

History of Catan


The person responsible for the hoards of Catan loyalists is Klaus Teuber. Teuber is the creator of The Settlers of Catan, a board game where players compete to establish the most successful colony in the fictional island of Catan. Since its inception in Germany 1995, Catan has sold more than 18 million copies worldwide. Before Catan's success, Teuber was working as a dental technician outside the industrial city of Darmstadt, Germany. He was unhappy with his current occupation at the time and started creating board games in his basement as a form of escapism.

This was my own world I created. -Klaus Teuber

In 1998, Teuber left his job in in the dental lab as he felt Catan was sufficient enought to support him and his family. In 2002, he made it into a family business. Teuber's sons, Guido and Benjamin are now included as managing directors. Guido focusses on the English-speaking market and Benjamin primarily focuses on the international game development. Today, Catan is now available in over 40 languages. According to the official Catan website, approximately 20 million people regularly spend time in the Catan gaming universe.

Rules of Catan


Game Components


  • 19 terrain tiles shaped liked hexagons
  • 18 circular number tokens
  • 95 resource cards(bearing the symbols for the brick, grain, lumber, one, wool resources)
  • 25 development cards
  • 16 cities
  • 20 settlements(shaped like houses)
  • 60 roads
  • 2 dice
  • 1 robber

Each player chooses a color and takes its corresponding pieces: 5 settlements, 4 cities, 15 roads. Every player places two settlements and two roads on the board. Catan has three main gameplay components: producing resources, trading resources, and using those resources to build things.

To produce resources, each player rolls two dice. If any player has a settlement adjacent to a tile marked with a token that matches the total dice roll. Players receive one resource card for each adjacent settlement.

To trade resouces with other players, talk with fellow players to see if anyone would like to make a deal. Perhaps, someone would like to trade a Sheep for Wood? If other players would not like to partake in a trade, players can always trade four of any one resource for a single card. During a player's turn, other players can negotiate with the player who's turn it is. However, they cannot negotiate with each other until it is their turn.

To determine what it costs to build look at the construction costs reference card. For example, to build a road it would take 1 brick and 1 wood. A player can build as many items as they want during their turn provided they have enough resources.

The first player to reach 10 Victory Points wins. There are several different ways to gain Victory Points:

  1. Building Settlements
  2. Upgrading Settlements to Cities
  3. Building the longest road of at least five segments
  4. Raising the largest army of at least three Knight cards
  5. Holding Victory Point Cards in your hand

Best Winning Strategies


Strategy 1 - Pick Brick and Wood:Brick and wood are the most importan resources on the board. It is important to keep this in mind when picking where to place your settlements. Having an abundance of each item can be advantageous for building cities but also for negotiationg among other players.

Strategy 2 - Count the dots: Count the dots: If you are unsure where to place your next settlement, count the number of dots beneath the numbers on the tiles. The sum of the surrounding dots is the total number of chances in 36 tries that the player has of collecting the resource. So, drop your Settlements in the intersection with the most total dots on the three adjacent hexes.

Strategy 3 - Luck: Catan although a game of strategy is also a game that involves a lot of luck. The following table displays the most likely dice rolls. Therefore, one strategy would be to place your settlements on the tiles that have the highest possibility of being rolled. However, beware that the most popular numbers rolled are high targets for robbers!
